Core Specialty

Energy-Based Devices

Energy-based devices — electrosurgical units, RF ablation systems, microwave platforms, ultrasonic devices, and laser delivery systems — place some of the most demanding requirements on cables and connectors. High voltage, RF shielding, precise impedance matching, sterilization compatibility, and zero-failure safety standards all apply simultaneously. This is Gii's deepest area of specialization, and where our engineering team delivers the most consistent cost reduction.

  • Electrosurgical unit (ESU) cables
  • RF & microwave delivery assemblies
  • Ultrasonic device cables
  • Laser fiber delivery systems
  • High-voltage rated per IEC 60601
  • EMI/RFI shielded designs
  • Single-use & reusable configurations
  • DFM review included on every program
Custom Connectors

Custom Medical Connectors

Expensive branded off-the-shelf connectors work — but they're priced for the brand, not the performance. If your device uses a premium push-pull connector simply because it was the easiest spec to write, you're likely leaving significant margin on the table at scale. Gii engineers purpose-built alternatives in push-pull, locking, non-locking, and breakaway configurations that match or exceed clinical performance at a fraction of the branded cost — with full drop-in compatibility where required.

  • Non-locking push-pull designs
  • Bayonet & screw-locking styles
  • 1–30+ contacts per design
  • Purpose-built alternatives to expensive off-the-shelf connectors
  • Biocompatible materials
  • Single-use & reusable options
  • Custom overmolded housings
  • Fully proprietary architectures
RF Ablation

RF Ablation Assemblies

RF ablation cables and connectors sit at the intersection of high voltage, precise signal integrity, and clinical safety — and the cost per unit compounds fast for single-use programs. Gii has deep experience in the specific IEC 60601 compliance requirements for RF ablation assemblies, and our DFM engineers consistently find simplifications — connector configurations, insulation materials, contact count — that reduce per-unit cost without affecting the clinical outcome.

  • Overmolded banana plug configs
  • Push-pull RF connectors (1–30+ contacts)
  • Breakaway connectors for pain probes
  • High-voltage rated per IEC 60601
  • Low-leakage RF insulation
  • Full DHR/DHF documentation per program
Scopes & Probes

Scopes, Probes & Catheters

In colonoscopes, laparoscopes, and ultrasound probes, the cable and connector are frequently the highest-cost component in the assembly — and often the most over-specified. Gii engineers have deep experience in the signal integrity, flexibility, and sterilization requirements these devices demand, and apply rigorous DFM to identify where those specs can be met at meaningfully lower cost without compromising imaging quality or procedural safety.

  • Colonoscope & laparoscope cables
  • Ultrasound & imaging cables
  • Multi-lumen catheter assemblies
  • RF & microwave delivery
  • Sterilization-rated flexible tubing
  • Shielded EMI/RFI designs
Pain Management

Pain Management Devices

Single-use RF ablation programs for interventional pain live or die on per-unit cost. Breakaway connectors, high-voltage insulation, and sterilization-compatible packaging all need to be engineered to single-use economics — not specified like a reusable device. Gii's engineers know exactly where the cost is in these assemblies, and how to remove it while keeping the high-voltage safety requirements that make the procedure viable.

  • Single-use RF ablation probes
  • Purpose-built breakaway connectors
  • Sterilization-compatible packaging
  • High-voltage RF insulation
  • Precision contact alignment
  • Operator-safety engineered

  • Surgical handpiece assemblies
  • RF ablation probe connections
  • Imaging & visualization cables
  • Drop-in connector replacements
  • Supply chain consolidation
  • Single-use & reusable versions
  • Production to 20,000+ units/week
