Design-Driven Cost Reduction — The Gii Difference

The single biggest driver of cable and connector cost in medical devices isn't manufacturing overhead — it's the choice of connector itself. When OEMs default to branded off-the-shelf connectors like LEMO, ODU, or Fischer, they pay a significant price premium that compounds across every unit shipped. Gii's engineers design purpose-built custom connector solutions that meet your exact performance and regulatory requirements at a fraction of that cost. Paired with rigorous DFM reviews and material optimization on every program, that design-first approach is how Gii consistently delivers meaningful cost reductions for our OEM partners.

Custom Connector Design & Engineering

This is the core of what Gii does and where the greatest cost savings are found. Rather than specifying expensive branded connectors, our engineers design purpose-built alternatives — push-pull, locking, non-locking, and breakaway configurations — that match your clinical and regulatory requirements at a dramatically lower per-unit cost. Biocompatible, durable, and engineered from the ground up for your specific device.

  • Biocompatible material selection and validation
  • Push-pull, locking, and non-locking configurations
  • Backward compatible design engineering
  • Miniaturization and tight tolerance capabilities
  • Full DFM review and process validation

  • 100% electrical test coverage
  • Full traceability and documentation

Connector Electronics & PCBA

Surface-mount and through-hole PCB assembly for the electronics that live inside and around your cable and connector assemblies. IPC Class 2/3 standards with full component traceability, automated optical inspection (AOI), and X-ray inspection — precision electronics as part of a fully integrated cable and connector build.

  • SMT and through-hole assembly
  • IPC Class 2/3 standards compliance
  • Full component traceability
  • AOI and X-ray inspection
  • Medical-grade quality assurance

Metal Stamping & Contact Fabrication

Precision stamping and CNC machining of connector contacts, pins, shields, and housings to tight tolerances and high volume. Part of the custom connector design process — engineering the specific metal contact geometry your device needs rather than defaulting to a standard component that may be over-specified and overpriced.

  • Precision stamping and forming
  • CNC machining capabilities
  • Connector contacts and shields
  • Tight tolerances at volume scale
  • Material expertise and supply chain

Coating & Surface Treatment

Lubricity, biocompatible, and electrical performance coatings applied to cables and connectors with precision and validated consistency. Material and coating specifications are reviewed as part of our DFM process — ensuring you're using the right coating for your application, not an over-specified one that inflates your unit cost.

  • Lubricity coatings for device insertion

  • Electrical performance coatings
  • Single-use and reusable device support
